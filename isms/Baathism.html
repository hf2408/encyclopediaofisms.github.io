Baathism

Baathism is a political ideology that emerged in the mid-20th century in the Arab world, particularly associated with Syria and Iraq. It emphasizes Arab nationalism, socialism, and pan-Arabism, aiming for the unification of Arab nations into a single state. The Baath Party, a key proponent of Baathism, advocated for secularism, social justice, and the promotion of Arab culture and identity. However, the implementation of Baathist policies varied between countries and faced criticism for authoritarianism and human rights abuses.
